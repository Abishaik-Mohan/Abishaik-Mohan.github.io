Machine Learning Research Intern

Indian Institute of Technology, Madras

My Work

Working under the tutelage of Dr. Harish Guruprasad from the CS Department and under an experienced Carnatic vocalist, I researched modeling musical attributes to classify Carnatic ragas and eventually build a Smart-music Tutor to provide real-time recommendations for user/instrument voice.

My work involved developing a GUI with Tkinter to detect user/instrument audio and pre-processing it using Music21 toolkit to provide recommendations for improvement or alternate phrases in the event of a mistake. The pre-processed audio was fed into Music Information Retrieval (MIR) toolbox, written in MATLAB, which extracts the pitch, tonality, rythm etc.

I experimented with several ML classification algorithms such as C4.5, Bayesian, Random Forest and K*. Ultimately, K* model achieved the highest accuracy of 94.72% and a Kappa statistic value of 0.91.

It was followed by Random Forest with 92.64%, then C4.5 with 89.70% and then Bayesian classifier with accuracy of 86.76%. I observed that Random Forest classifier gives better accuracy as compared to K* when instances were less. But when the number of instances gets increased, K* performs better than Random Forest!

Tech

Tkinter, K* classifier, Music21 tooklit, MIR toolbox, Modeling, Machine Learning
